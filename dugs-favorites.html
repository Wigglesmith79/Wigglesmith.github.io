    Games

  • Video Games
    1. Deaths Door
      • Death's Door is a 3D, isometric action-adventure game. The player starts with a sword, and bow and arrow. Sword strikes string together into combos and ammo for the bow is replenished by using the sword. Four other weapons and three magic projectiles become available as the game progresses- wikipedia
      • You wont find this on many top games list but its near the top on mine, it is just fun and not too hard to make me want to put the game down, something I dealt with several times in Hollow Knight. The combat is tight and simple, the exploration is optional but rewarding, making it very replayable. The visuals all just support the gamplay, only one attack in the entire game felt bad/ unfair to me.
    2. Spiderheck
      • This the one piece of media that has given me the most fun and laughs out of any other, you start feeling like a newborn giraffe and end like John Wick. The physics baised gameplay make things fun and goofy but also consistant so you can master them. Spiderheck also has tons of customizability keeping it fresh for years
    3. Downwell
      • Downwell is a mobile game with random level generation, permadeath and upgrades that has a vertical format where you fall down a hole stomping and shooting enemies building up a combo and getting upgrades. The game has extreemly tight design, because nothing is waisted and every game elemnt including the art has multiple uses. its is the only game that I am actually good at, I've beat it on normal and hard modes.
    4. Valheim
      • Valheim is a survival crafting game with a viking theme and a early 2000s low-poly style. The game has pretty good progresion and a great building system where things you build actually look good. When I first played the game it was the first game in a long time that had me thinking about it while I wasent playing it.
